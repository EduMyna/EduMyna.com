## SLIDE 2: Where It Started (2009)

**"What if Kerala could be India's Silicon Coast?"**

**2009:** MobME proposed India's first Public-Private-Partnership incubator
**The Vision:** Enable entrepreneurs. Build a startup ecosystem from scratch.
**The Commitment:** 10,000 sq ft space, ₹2.5 crore grant, support 48 startups over five years

**What Actually Happened:**
- Supported 500 startups (10x the commitment)
- Created 3,139 jobs
- Alumni raised ₹45 Crores
- ROI: For every ₹1 by government, ₹9.68 invested by private investors

**Visual:** Early Startup Village campus photo — hundreds of young founders, energy, momentum

---

## SLIDE 3: The Crack in the Foundation (2012-2013)

**The uncomfortable truth:** It was working... but it couldn't scale.

**What we built:**
- A thriving community ✓
- Real outcomes ✓
- Mission-driven culture ✓

**What we didn't build:**
- A system that worked without heroic individuals ✗
- Predictable, repeatable processes ✗
- A product that scaled beyond our physical presence ✗

**The Diagnosis (2015):**
*"We were helping people. But whether we were building something that could actually scale—that was a different question entirely."*

**The Decision:** Student startups only. Digital-first. Build a real platform.

---

## SLIDE 4: SV.CO — The Digital Transformation (2015-2017)

**February 2015:** Announced the shift. Physical operations wind down. Web-only platform launches.

**What We Built:**
- Timelines for every startup
- Progression models (5/10/20/30 stages)
- Structured cohort programs
- Submission-based progress tracking
- Weekly feedback loops

**The Real Learning:**
We stopped asking *"What do startup founders need?"*
Started asking *"What do students building their first product actually need?"*

**Answer:** They need someone to teach them how to build, not just tell them to build.

## SLIDE 6: 2018 — The Year of Real Experiments

**What we learned by doing:**

### **Hackkar (Developer Hiring)**
- Thesis: Help tier 2/3 college developers demonstrate skills
- Result: Learned what students valued vs what companies needed
- Closed: October 2018 (timing/market mismatch)

### **Apple iOS @ Manipal**
- Credit-integrated university program
- Students took our course as elective
- **This model worked.** We'd build on this.

### **Facebook VR Partnership**
- Facebook-funded, School of Innovation program
- Delivered, but mission misalignment showed
- Learned: When funder doesn't own mission, things drift

### **Freshworks Partnership (Sastra University)**
- Mission-aligned, curriculum open-sourced
- Genuine partnership, sustainable outcomes
- **Lesson: Mission alignment is operational, not optional**

## SLIDE 7: Pupilfirst Emerges (2018-2021)

**The Realization (Nov-Dec 2018):**
The platform we'd built was comparable to Teachable.com (valued $100M+).
We could separate brand from framework → turn it into SaaS.

**December 5, 2018:** pupilfirst.com domain registered

**What We Built:**
- School administration interface
- Rich curriculum editor (like Medium/WordPress Gutenberg)
- Cohort management at scale
- Submission tracking & feedback systems
- Complete LMS infrastructure

**June 27, 2019:** Major milestone — 316 commits merged in one day
Student dashboard fully replaced. New curriculum interface live.

**2020: Open-Sourced Everything**
- Full platform
- All features
- Universities could self-host

**Result:** Gained trust instead of losing customers.
Universities wanted to partner, not just purchase.

## SLIDE 10: RealWorldReady — Where Everything Came Together

**The Philosophy:**
*"First iteration is a shitty first draft. Second iteration is where design shows."*

**The Program:** RealWorldReady
Not about startups. Not about developer skills.
About **agency.** Teaching students how to create opportunities instead of waiting for them.

**The Approach:**
- Practice-heavy (80% doing, 20% consuming)
- Students actually ship work, not just listen
- Systems thinking for careers
- Real transformation through iteration

**The Outcomes:**
- **63% rated sessions 10/10**
- Students reported clarity on goals, confidence to act, improved collaboration
- 30+ sessions developed
- 1,500+ learners
- Clients: McDonald's, IMG Academy, Glovo, FOSS United, ed-tech unicorns

**The Gap:**
Pedagogy was tested. Outcomes were proven.
**But to scale to millions — I needed infrastructure.**

## SLIDE 14: What We're Not

Let me be clear about what EduMyna is **NOT:**

❌ **Not a VC-funded startup**
We're Section 8 (nonprofit with revenue generation). We're building for sustainability, not exits.

❌ **Not another certificate mill**
We care about capability, not certificates. Practice-heavy programs, real outcomes.

❌ **Not proprietary platform vendors**
We're open-source by default. Universities own their infrastructure.

❌ **Not trying to "disrupt" educators**
We're trying to give educators the operational infrastructure to do their work at scale.

❌ **Not grant-dependent**

## SLIDE 18: What I've Learned About What Works

**10 lessons from 12 years:**

1. **Platforms scale. Communities don't.** Build systems, not heroics.
2. **Skills first, outcomes second.** Capability is the only real moat.
3. **Mission alignment is operational.** Choose partners who own the mission.
4. **University integration > standalone.** Credit beats certificates.
5. **Open-source changes incentives.** Trust beats proprietary moats.
6. **Practice beats content.** 80% doing, 20% consuming.
7. **Specificity unlocks strategy.** Solve one problem deeply.
8. **Systems > features.** Think incentives, not interfaces.
9. **Policy is product work.** To reach millions, change frameworks.
10. **Structure = strategy.** Section 8 unlocks the right partnerships.

**Everything we're building incorporates these lessons.**
