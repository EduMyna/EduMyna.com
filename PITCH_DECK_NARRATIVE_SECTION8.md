## SLIDE 2: THE PROBLEM - Education That Fails Students

### The Reality:
- **200 million** young Indians will enter the workforce in the next decade
- College degrees don't translate to capability
- Students lack agency, career navigation skills, real-world experience
- Attendance policies prioritize compliance over learning
- Theoretical content disconnected from careers
- No support for developing initiative and problem-solving

### The System Is Broken:
- Universities optimize for compliance, not capability
- Students graduate with degrees but no practical skills
- Employers complain: "We can't find capable people"
- Students complain: "No one taught us how to actually navigate careers"

### The Gap:
**Students need transformation, not just information.**

They need:
- Agency (how to create opportunities, not wait)
- Systems thinking (understanding career patterns)
- Practice-heavy learning (ship real work, not just assignments)
- Feedback loops that arrive when useful
- Structure that holds them accountable

**But the infrastructure to deliver this at scale doesn't exist.**

## SLIDE 7: BUSINESS MODEL (Revenue-Generating, Sustainable)

### Revenue Streams (Not VC-Dependent):

**1. University Partnerships (B2B2C)**
- Universities pay per student for credit-integrated courses
- Example: Apple iOS @ Manipal, Freshworks @ Sastra
- Pricing: ₹5,000-15,000 per student (scaled pricing)
- Target: 10 universities, 500 students/university/year = ₹2.5-7.5Cr/year

**2. Corporate-Sponsored Programs (B2B)**
- Companies sponsor programs to build talent pipelines
- Example: Freshworks SEDG (scholarships for disadvantaged students)
- Pricing: ₹10-50 lakhs per cohort (depending on scale)
- Target: 5-10 corporate partners/year = ₹1-5Cr/year

**3. Direct Student Programs (B2C)**
- Students pay directly for RealWorldReady bootcamps/courses
- Pricing: ₹2,000-10,000 per student (accessible pricing)
- Target: 1,000-5,000 students/year = ₹20L-5Cr/year

**4. Government Contracts (B2G)**
- State governments fund skill development programs
- Example: Rajasthan partnership (2018)
- Pricing: Program-based (₹50L-2Cr per program)
- Target: 2-3 state partnerships/year = ₹1-6Cr/year

**5. LMS Hosting & Support (SaaS)**
- Universities/institutions want hosted Pupilfirst (don't want to self-host)
- Pricing: ₹5-20 lakhs/year per institution (tiered)
- Target: 20 institutions = ₹1-4Cr/year

### Projected Revenue (Year 2-3):
- Conservative: ₹5-10 Cr/year
- Optimistic: ₹15-25 Cr/year

### Cost Structure:
- **Program Delivery:** 40-50% (funding Second Design, Tinkera, facilitators)
- **Technology:** 15-20% (LMS development, hosting, tools)
- **Marketing & Acquisition:** 15-20% (bringing students to programs)
- **Operations & Admin:** 10-15%
- **Reinvestment:** 10-20% (surplus goes back into programs, infrastructure)

### Why This Works:
- **No dependency on grants** (self-sustaining after initial funding)
- **Scalable model** (more universities = more revenue, same infrastructure)
- **Mission-aligned** (higher revenue = more students reached)
- **Section 8 structure** (tax benefits, reinvest surplus)

**Revenue is how we survive. Impact is why we exist.**

## SLIDE 14: RISKS & MITIGATION

### Key Risks:

**1. University Adoption Speed**
- Risk: Universities slow to adopt new platforms
- Mitigation: Start with proven relationships (Manipal, Sastra), credit-integrated model already tested

**2. Revenue Ramp-Up Time**
- Risk: Takes 12-18 months to hit revenue targets
- Mitigation: Initial funding covers 24 months, multiple revenue streams reduce dependency

**3. Program Quality at Scale**
- Risk: Quality drops as we scale facilitators
- Mitigation: Detailed facilitator guides, train-the-trainer model, feedback loops

**4. Competitive Landscape**
- Risk: Other ed-tech platforms (BYJU'S, Unacademy, etc.)
- Mitigation: We're infrastructure (B2B/B2G), not direct-to-consumer; open-source moat

**5. Regulatory (Section 8 Compliance)**
- Risk: Section 8 reporting requirements, compliance burden
- Mitigation: Hire compliance expert, transparent metrics from day one

**6. Dependency on Key Person (Gautam)**
- Risk: Founder single point of failure
- Mitigation: Build team, document processes, open-source ensures continuity
